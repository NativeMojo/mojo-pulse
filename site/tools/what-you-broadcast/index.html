What you broadcast

Your Mac, as strangers see it.

Everyone on the same Wi-Fi can learn things about your Mac with a browse and a quick scan. Pulse shows you that view — what you're revealing — so nothing on the list is a surprise.

Real screenshot from the current release — addresses blurred. Click to zoom.

Reachable services, honestly

The headline is the one you want green: whether any sharing service — files, screen, remote login — is reachable from the network at all. Below it, every listener the network can see, so “nothing to see here” is verified, not assumed.

The AirDrop stance

AirDrop set to “Everyone” means nearby strangers can send you files. macOS quietly reverts it to Contacts Only after ten minutes, but if you flipped it recently, this is the reminder to flip it back.

What pairing discloses

Your paired Bluetooth accessories are part of your Mac's broadcast personality too. Seeing the list plainly — headphones, watches, the lot — is the first step to deciding whether you're comfortable with it.

Under the hoodRead-only, on device. This tool observes what your Mac exposes; it changes nothing and sends nothing.
