Network activity

See every connection your Mac makes.

At any moment your Mac holds dozens of connections you never see. Pulse shows them live — as a searchable list, or as pins on a map of the world — and names the app behind every single one.

Live capture from the app.

A map, not a mystery

Connections cluster into pins by location, with live counts and your traffic ticking in the corner. Hover a pin and Pulse tells you exactly which processes are talking to that corner of the internet — the “what is my Mac saying to Frankfurt?” question, answered in one hover.

Attributed to the app responsible

Every connection is tied to its process at the socket level. Flip to the list for the full table — search by process, address, or country, filter by state, and click through to the process inspector when something deserves a closer look.

Flags when it matters

Turn on connection alerts and Pulse checks sustained connections against threat intelligence — a flagged destination raises a card, and an app talking to a country it never has before gets a quiet journal note. Never a bare “new IP” alarm; only things worth your time.

Under the hoodSocket enumeration and process attribution happen on device. The map's geo lookup is opt-in and sends only public IP addresses — your LAN never leaves the Mac. Results are cached locally.
